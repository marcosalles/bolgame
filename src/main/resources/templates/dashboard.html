Instructions

  1. The players:

    • The game is played by two players (P1 and P2)
    • Each player owns six pits and a single big pit

  2. The board:

    • The board is divided in half between the two players
    • The pits owned by P1 are: the big pit to the left and the six top pits in the middle
    • The pits owned by P2 are: the big pit to the right and the six bottom pits in the middle
    • The pits are ordered in a counter-clockwise manner, so a player's first pit is always the farthest one from it's own big pit
    • The big pits start empty and all the other pits start with 6 marbles each
    • The amount of marbles in a pit is represented by the number written at the bottom of the pit
    • A non-empty pit has the visual aid of a drawing with marbles on it

  3. The game:

    • The objective of the game is to have the most marbles in your own big pit when it ends
    • P1 and P2 are randomized upon creation of the game and P1 always starts
    • A move consists of chosing a pit. When you click on it you sow all the marbles in that pit in a counter-clockwise manner, dropping one marble in each of the pits
    • If you pass through your opponent's big pit while sowing your marbles, skip it
    • If, during a move, the last marble sown falls in your own big pit, you get an extra turn. This has no limit and can be repeated indefinitely
    • If, during a move, the last marble sown falls in an empty pit of your own, you capture the opponent's opposite pit's marbles and place them in your own pit
    • The game ends when any one player's pits are empty and the player can't make a move
    • When the game ends, all remaining marbles not in a big pit are moved to that player's big pit
